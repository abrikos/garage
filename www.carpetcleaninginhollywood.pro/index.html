Rugs and carpets make any house more comfortable!

It's surely homier with rugs or carpets on the floor. And not only homier but actually it’s much more pleasant to walk on rugs and carpets than on cold stone or tiles. Unfortunately, exactly because we walk on these rugs every day, they don’t get that “good as new” look when we clean them, unlike our curtains. No matter how much you try, there is always some dirt and lint or hair somewhere hidden in the corner and every day you think: “Tomorrow I’ll take care of it.”

But ‘tomorrow’ never happens because cleaning a rug or a carpet is a boring and tedious chore which nobody’s willing to do unless they are paid for it.

Even if you are willing to fight for the cleanliness of the rug or carpet every day, you have to take several factors into consideration. You have to know what detergent to use, what material your rug is made of and how to dry it properly. Otherwise, you’ll have to pay a price for your mistakes and in the case of expensive rugs it’s not cheap. Maybe in this case, it’s better to trust professionals to take care of it.

Risks of unprofessional cleaning

A powerful vacuum cleaner is able to remove about 60% of dust, but the remaining 40% penetrate deep into the fibers and form a ‘risk zone’. The ‘risk zone’ provides a breeding ground for dust mites, which are sources of allergens. Over time, the use of these carpets might lead to health problems, and wet cleaning with household chemicals only aggravates the situation.

Rescue don’t ruin

“What doesn’t kill you makes you stronger” works for dirt in the carpet or rug too. Using detergents, inexperienced cleaners "push" the dirt deeper and make it impossible to clean it at home. Some veteran stains need professional cleaning only.
